Janaki TEMPLE

Sitting in the Dhanusha District of south-east Nepal, Janakpur, also known as Janakpurdham, is a historically and culturally significant city. It is very sacred in Hindu mythology, having been the birthplace of Goddess Sita and the location of her marriage to Lord Rama. The majestic Janaki Mandir and the Ram Mandir are two of the city's well-known temples. Recognised for its festivals, customary art, and delectable food, Janakpurdham, the erstwhile capital of Mithila, is a thriving centre of Maithili culture. Because it offers a unique fusion of spiritual legacy and cultural experiences, well-connected by road, train, and air, Janakpurdham draws pilgrims and visitors.

Story behind janaki temple

The Janaki Mandir, also known as the Janaki Temple, is one of the most revered and significant temples in Nepal, dedicated to Goddess Sita, also known as Janaki. The story behind this temple is deeply intertwined with the epic Ramayana and local legends. The Janaki Mandir was constructed in 1910 by Queen Vrisha Bhanu of Tikamgarh, India.
